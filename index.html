Colourblindness in a nutshell

What is colourblindness?

Colourblindness is when you can't see certain colours, or distinguish between them.

There are many types, the most common one is red-green colourblindness. The reason why men are more likely to be colourblind is because they only have one X chromosone, so if one carries the colourblindness trait then then will have it, but as women carry two X chromosones so even if one of the chromosones have the trait, the other will override it.

How many people are colourblind?

Approximately 1 in 12 males and 1 in 200 females are colourblind (8 and 0.5% respectively) (source)
